How It Works

Three steps, 20–40 seconds. Powered by Claude AI.

1

Read & Summarize Claude AI

Claude reads the full article and produces a concise executive summary capturing the main argument and key assertions — calibrated to article complexity.

2

Structure the Detailed Breakdown Claude AI

The article is decomposed into named sections — Context / Background, Key Findings, and Implications — so you can see what's established, what's new, and what it means.

3

Assess Political Lean Claude AI

Claude evaluates the article's framing, language choices, and source selection to assess political lean — with a high bar for attribution. Center is the default unless evidence requires otherwise.

Political Lean Tiers

Each article gets one of five lean ratings, plus a confidence indicator (High or Low) reflecting how strongly the evidence supports the assessment.

Left

Clear progressive framing, source selection, and language choices.

Center-Left

Mild progressive lean detectable in tone or framing.

Center

Balanced reporting without partisan framing — the default.

Center-Right

Mild conservative lean detectable in tone or framing.

Right

Clear conservative framing, source selection, and language choices.

Center is the default. News-Distiller only flags a non-Center lean when there is direct, observable evidence in the article — specific phrasing, framing, source choices, or omissions. This high evidentiary bar prevents over-attribution of bias and keeps the political lean indicator meaningful.

🌐 Web App — Backup Option

If the Chrome extension isn't ideal for your situation, the web app provides the same distillation on any browser.

Use News-Distiller on the web

Open app.news-distiller.com in any browser, paste an article URL or text, and get the same structured distillation as the extension. Your Distiller Pack for Chrome code also works in the web app — they share the same balance.

⚠️ Paywalled articles will not load via URL in the web app (NYT, WSJ, Washington Post, etc.). For those, copy the article text and paste it into the Paste Article Text field. The Chrome extension reads paywalled pages directly because it operates on the page already loaded in your browser.

On iPhone or iPad, you can add a Safari Share Sheet shortcut to send articles to the web app with one tap. Set up the iOS shortcut →
